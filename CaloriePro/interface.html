Basic concept of fitness

◉ Calories:

A unit for measuring how much energy food will produce. Every food have some calories.

Example:

  • One whole egg - 155 cal
  • One chapati - 71 cal
  • 100g of peanut - 567 cal

◉ Maintenance Calories:

Maintenance calories are the number of calories that won't cause weight loss or weight gain - they serve to maintain the current state.

Example:

  • Let the boy name is Santhosh.
  • His weight is 75 Kg.
  • Then his maintenance calories is 2350 cal.

◉ Calorie Surplus:

A caloric surplus is when the number of calories that we consume is higher than the number of calories we burn. In other words, Eating more calories than maintenance calories. In a caloric surplus, our body mass increases.

Example:

  • Santhosh want to gain weight.
  • His goal weight is 80 Kg.
  • Then his surplus calories is 2850 cal.

◉ Calorie Deficit:

A caloric deficit is when the number of calories that we consume is lower than the number of calories we burn. In other words, Eating less calories than maintenance caloriesIn a caloric deficit, our body mass decreases.

Example:

  • Santhosh want to loss weight.
  • His goal weight is 70 Kg.
  • Then his deficit calories is 1950 cal.
